# Resources

## Academic Papers (Foundational)
- Browne, C. et al. (2012). *A Survey of Monte Carlo Tree Search Methods.* IEEE Transactions on Computational Intelligence and AI in Games. — The definitive survey paper covering all major MCTS variants.
- Kocsis, L. & Szepesvári, C. (2006). *Bandit Based Monte-Carlo Planning.* ECML 2006. — The paper that introduced UCT.

## Books
- Sutton, R. & Barto, A. (2018). *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (2nd ed.). MIT Press. — Chapters on Monte Carlo methods and planning provide important context.
- Russell, S. & Norvig, P. (2021). *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.). — Section on MCTS in game-playing.

## Online / Interactive
- [MCTS AI — a visual Tic-Tac-Toe MCTS walkthrough](https://playground.paperspace.io/mcts/) — Interactive visualizer of the tree-building process.
- [Swiechowski et al., "MCTS: A Survey" — ArXiv version](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.04784) — More recent survey covering enhancements and applications.

## Communities
- **r/reinforcementlearning** on Reddit — Active community for general RL and MCTS discussions.
- **AI Game Programming mailing lists** / **GDC Vault** — for game-engine-specific architecture patterns.
